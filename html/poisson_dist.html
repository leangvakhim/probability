The Poisson Distribution

A Step-by-Step Interactive Guide

1. What is it?

Imagine you run a small coffee shop. You want to know: "How many customers will walk through the door in the next hour?"

The Poisson Distribution is a mathematical concept that helps predict the probability of a given number of events happening in a fixed interval of time or space.

2. The Magic Number: Lambda ($\lambda$)

The entire Poisson distribution is built around just one single number: Lambda ($\lambda$).

$\lambda$ represents the average rate of occurrences. If you usually get an average of 4 customers an hour, then $\lambda = 4$. But remember, "average" doesn't mean exact! In any given hour, you might get 2, or maybe 7.

Try sliding it! Notice how the curve shifts right and flattens out.

Interpretation: If $\lambda$ is 4, the highest bar (most likely outcome) is around 4. But there's still a chance of getting 0, or 10!

3. When can we use it?

You can't use the Poisson Distribution for just anything. It only works if your real-world scenario meets three strict assumptions:

1

Independence

One event doesn't affect the next. If one customer walks in, it doesn't make it more or less likely for another to immediately follow.

2

Constant Rate

The average rate ($\lambda$) remains constant during the interval. A coffee shop rush hour (variable rate) would violate this!

3

Singularity

Events occur one at a time. Two events cannot happen at the exact same fraction of a second.

Other examples where Poisson works well: Number of typos on a book page, number of meteors seen in an hour, number of emails received per day.

4. Recap & The Math

To recap: The Poisson Distribution calculates the probability of exactly $k$ events happening in an interval, given an average rate of $\lambda$.

Here is the formal equation (Probability Mass Function) that generated all those bar charts:

$$P(X = k) = \frac{\lambda^k e^{-\lambda}}{k!}$$
